## The objective

The goal is not to collect every possible reward. It is to know your **real final cost** before buying and to avoid relying on rewards that may not track.

A clean stack can include:

1. A public sale price.
2. A valid store coupon.
3. A card-linked offer.
4. A cashback portal that allows the coupon used.
5. Store points earned for a future purchase.

> Never count a future reward as an instant discount. Keep "paid today" and "future value" separate.

## Build the stack in order

### 1. Verify the base offer

Open the product in a clean browser tab and record the item, exact variant, seller, shipping cost, and current price.

```text
Item price:      $50.00
Shipping:         $0.00
Tax:              $3.18
Paid today:      $53.18
```

### 2. Read the coupon terms

Check minimum spend, eligible products, expiration, account restrictions, and whether the code blocks portal credit.

### 3. Activate the portal last

After you finish comparing offers, start a fresh session through the selected portal. Do not open competing portal links afterward.

### 4. Save proof

Keep the order confirmation, activated offer, and portal click record until the reward becomes payable.

## Calculate the honest result

```text
Paid today                 $53.18
Confirmed statement credit -$10.00
Pending portal cashback     -$4.00
Future store points         -$5.00
----------------------------------
Projected net cost          $34.18
```

Label pending value as pending. A screenshot is not a payout.

## Stop conditions

Skip the purchase when the deal only makes sense if every uncertain reward tracks, the return policy is poor, or you would not buy the item without the stack.
